# Executive Dashboard Story
**Retail Performance Review — FY 2024–2025**
*Prepared for: Leadership Team*
*Author: Bhoomi Solanki (2511412)*

---

## Executive Summary

Over FY 2024–2025, our retail operations generated approximately **Rs 217 million in total sales** and **Rs 33.3 million in total profit**, resulting in an overall profit margin of **15.3%**. The business shows healthy top-line growth with strong Technology category performance, reliable South and North regional dominance, and an emerging Home Office segment opportunity.

However, three interconnected risks require immediate leadership attention: **Furniture's structural underperformance**, the **damaging effect of high discounts on profit**, and a **4.5% return rate** concentrated in Furniture. Addressing these three areas together could meaningfully improve net profitability without requiring additional sales volume.

---

## What Is Performing Well

**Technology is the profit engine.** With Rs 153.9 million in sales and Rs 28 million in profit, Technology accounts for over 84% of total profit. Sub-categories like Copiers, Accessories, and Phones consistently generate margins above 17–18%, well above the company average. This category is also less discount-sensitive and has the lowest return rate (2%) across all categories. Technology should be prioritized in every marketing channel.

**South and North regions are outperforming.** South leads in both sales (Rs 64.7M) and profit (Rs 10.0M), followed closely by North (Rs 54.6M sales, Rs 8.3M profit). These regions demonstrate strong market maturity and healthy per-order economics. Their performance validates current sales and distribution models in these geographies.

**Home Office segment shows the highest profitability.** Despite roughly equal sales across all three segments (~Rs 70–74M each), Home Office customers generate the best margins (Rs 11.6M profit vs Rs 10.7M for Corporate). This segment tends toward high-value, low-volume purchases — a favorable profile for margin expansion.

**Organic and Referral channels deliver quality traffic.** Campaign channels that rely on trust and word-of-mouth (Organic, Referral) correlate with lower return rates and higher customer satisfaction scores. These channels are cost-efficient relative to Paid campaigns.

---

## What Is Underperforming

**Furniture is a structural margin problem.** Generating only 6.9% profit margin (versus 18.2% for Technology), Furniture is the weakest-performing category by a wide margin. Tables and Bookcases are the worst sub-categories, generating less than 6% margins on combined sales of Rs 25.4 million. Large physical products carry higher logistics, storage, damage, and return costs that the current pricing does not adequately absorb.

**East and West regions lag behind.** East (Rs 48.9M, Rs 7.6M profit) and West (Rs 48.9M, Rs 7.4M profit) both trail South significantly. West in particular shows the lowest profit despite comparable sales to East, suggesting either higher cost structures or deeper discounting in that geography.

**Standard Class shipping is a missed experience opportunity.** Standard Class, which handles nearly 60% of all orders by value, averages 4.7 delivery days. In an era of rising customer delivery expectations, this gap versus Same Day (0.4 days) and First Class (1.8 days) could be eroding customer satisfaction and contributing to returns.

---

## What Risks Are Visible

**Discount erosion is measurable and worsening.** The correlation between discount rate and profit is negative (−0.27). Orders with discounts exceeding 20% consistently show compressed margins, and at 31–35% discount levels, profitability approaches or falls below zero. This is particularly acute in Furniture where base margins are already thin.

**Furniture return rate is a compounding cost.** With a return rate of ~5.7% (vs 2.0% for Technology), every Furniture return incurs reverse logistics, potential resale discounting, and customer service cost. On a 6.9% gross margin, these costs can easily wipe out unit economics on individual SKUs.

**Seasonal concentration risk.** Peak performance appears concentrated in Q3 and Q4, creating potential vulnerabilities in Q1 and Q2 — months when overstock from peak ordering can depress cash flow if inventory management is inadequate.

---

## What Opportunities Are Visible

**Home Office bundle strategy.** Home Office customers purchase across both Furniture and Technology. A curated "Home Office Bundle" (desk + monitor stand + peripherals) could increase average order value (AOV) by 15–25% per transaction while cross-subsidizing Furniture margin with Technology profitability.

**First Class shipping as a premium upsell.** First Class shipping at 1.8-day average delivery drives Rs 4.6M profit with strong margins. Offering First Class as a paid upgrade or free threshold reward (e.g., "Free upgrade on orders above Rs 25,000") could improve customer satisfaction, reduce return rates, and build loyalty.

**Referral and loyalty program expansion.** Organic and Referral channels deliver high-quality orders. A formal referral incentive program — particularly for Technology purchases — could cost-effectively expand the top of the funnel with customers who have higher lifetime value.

**East region market development.** East has the lowest sales volume but shows comparable profit margins to West. With targeted distribution investment, pricing alignment, and regional marketing, East represents the highest greenfield growth opportunity geographically.

---

## Recommended Business Actions

1. **Cap all discounts at 20%** across categories; restrict Furniture discounts to a maximum of 10%. Replace deep discounting with value-add offers (free accessories, extended warranty).

2. **Conduct a Furniture SKU audit** — discontinue or reprice any Furniture SKU with margin below 5% after returns. Prioritize Tables and Bookcases for renegotiation with suppliers.

3. **Launch a Home Office bundle program** in Q1 next year, targeting Technology + Furniture combinations with a 3–5% bundle discount that still yields higher margin than standalone Furniture sales.

4. **Invest in East region sales capability** — assign dedicated sales reps, run a regional demand generation campaign, and benchmark East state-level performance monthly.

5. **Upgrade Standard Class tracking and SLA commitments** — set an internal SLA of ≤4 days for Standard Class and monitor by region weekly. Automatically flag orders at risk of exceeding SLA for proactive customer communication.

6. **Build a formal referral rewards program** — offer Rs 500–1,000 cashback or discount to customers who refer verified new buyers. Track LTV by acquisition channel quarterly.

## Limitations of the Dashboard

- **No customer-level data**: The dataset contains order-level data. Without customer lifetime value (CLV) analysis, retention rates, or cohort data, it is difficult to measure true customer profitability.
- **No cost decomposition**: The "Cost" field (Sales − Profit) is a blended figure. It does not separate logistics cost, COGS, marketing spend, or overhead — limiting root cause analysis for low-margin categories.
- **Return reason not captured**: The dataset flags returns (1/0) but does not capture the reason for return (defective, size mismatch, changed mind). This limits the ability to design targeted return-reduction interventions.
- **Campaign attribution is partial**: 32 records have null campaign_channel values. Attribution analysis may undercount certain channel contributions.
- **Two-year dataset**: With only FY 2024–2025 data, long-term trend analysis and year-over-year growth benchmarking against prior periods is not possible.

---

## Suggested Next Analysis

1. **Customer Lifetime Value (CLV) segmentation** — if transaction-level customer history can be reconstructed, identify top-decile customers by lifetime spend and model their acquisition sources.
2. **Return reason analysis** — add a return reason code field to future datasets to enable targeted quality and experience improvements by SKU.
3. **Regional cost-to-serve analysis** — incorporate logistics cost data by region to calculate true net margin by geography.
4. **Time series forecasting** — with 24 months of data, an ARIMA or seasonal decomposition model could generate 6-month forward sales forecasts to guide inventory planning.
5. **A/B test on discount caps** — run a controlled experiment restricting discounts in one region vs baseline to measure the effect of discount policy on sales volume and net profit.
